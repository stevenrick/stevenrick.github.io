I am an AI systems architect, Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) researcher, and UX specialist currently working as a Solutions Architect at [NVIDIA](https://www.nvidia.com/). My core focus lies at the intersection of emerging machine learning technologies, human-centered design, and the development of collaborative generative tools that augment rather than replace human creativity.

Prior to joining NVIDIA, I served as a Postdoctoral Researcher at [MIT](https://web.mit.edu/), working conjointly with the [Center for Collective Intelligence (CCI)](https://cci.mit.edu/) and [CSAIL](https://www.csail.mit.edu/). I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science from [UC San Diego](https://ucsd.edu/), where my research was supported by the [Human-Centered Extended Intelligence (HXI)](https://hxi.ucsd.edu/) group and [DesignLab](https://designlab.ucsd.edu/). Throughout my career, I've actively sought to bridge the gap between academic theory and real-world deployment, steering projects as a Research Software Development Engineer at [Microsoft Research](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/) (EmpowerMD) and contributing to initiatives at [IBM Research](https://research.ibm.com/) (Project BlueSky), the [Veterans Medical Research Foundation](https://www.vmrf.org/), and CliniComp Inc.

Beyond my direct work and research, I am deeply committed to exploring algorithmic fairness and fostering inclusive computing environments. I helped Co-Organize [Dagstuhl Seminar 25442](https://www.dagstuhl.de/25442) directly analyzing the augmentation of human creativity with AI. I am also incredibly proud to have been a part of the foundational cohort of the [Cultural Competence in Computing (3C)](https://identity.cs.duke.edu/fellows.html) Fellowship. 

During my tenure at UC San Diego, I dedicated significant effort toward structural equity, working as a researcher for the [UnBIASED Initiative](https://www.unbiased.health/), sitting on the executive board for Graduate Women in Computing (GradWIC), and serving as a founding member of the CSE DEI Committee.
